Interest Differential

Interest Differencial is difference between two interest rates eg USD4.5% - AUD3.5% = 1% Differencial. Couple two currencies with different interest rate. It is always positive for the one with high rate.

The above image shows the central banks/commitment of traders/commercials the red line. Here is how to understand it. NB: Just because it is above zero basis line does not mean it is a buy or below zero basis line means it will go down/sell.

What we are looking for:

  • Highest Interest Differencial btw currencies example United States and Switzerland with 4.50% Differencial.
    Example: 4.50% - 0.00% = 4.50%
  • Net long/Above zero basis line by commercials or Net short/below zero basis line by commercials for individual coupled currencies.
  • To come up with quantitative analysis or buy/sell program check that coupled currencies have opposites side from zero basis line.

    Conclusion: USD is bulish if USD is above zero basis line and CHF is below
    zero basis line because USD has a higher interest rate against a weak CHF interest rate
  • Then analyse the individual chart to find market structure break.

Basically below zero basis line means commercials are saying the currency is overbought and price is likely to go down overtime. Basically above zero basis line means commercials are saying the currency is oversold and price is likely to go up overtime.




On interest chart you will see small upward movements on a single currency bearish trend, that is to show that smart money is hedging, Basically taking profits from their short sells previously. And vise verse



Note: The interest rate is not the only factor that determines the price of a currency. Other factors such as economic data, geopolitical events, and market sentiment also play a significant role.
